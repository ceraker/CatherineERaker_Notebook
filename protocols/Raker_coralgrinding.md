## Prada Lab Coral Grinding Protocol
### Performed by: Cassie Raker and Willow Dunster
### Samples: *Orbicella faveolata* fragments from coral dissertation research in La Parguera, Puerto Rico

##### Protocol 
1. Pre-chill tubes ground coral will be transferred into
2. Fill two coolers with dry ice
3. Remove samples from freezer and thaw them (just enough!) on ice
4. Place pre-chilled tubes in one cooler
5. Place pestle(s) and metal spatula(s) in other cooler
6. Select one sample, and use forceps to remove tissue
7. Place tissue in mortar
8. Pour in liquid nitrogen (enough to surround sample)
9. Let LN boil away
10. Take out chilled pestle and grind coral to powder
11. Use spatula to scrape powder into (labeled!) glass sample tube
12. Fill mortar with bleach and place forceps in mortar
13. Place pestle and spatula in waste container of bleach
14. Let sit until time to clean
15. To clean:
  1. Rinse 5 times with tap water
  2. Rinse 3 times with DI water
16. Dry all items and replace in coolers

##### Notes
- Use 10% bleach solution
- Useful to have more than one set of supplies, so you don't have to clean between every sample
