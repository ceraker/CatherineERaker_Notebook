## Prada Lab Chemical Extraction and Sample Preparation
### Performed by: Cassie Raker
### Modified from protocol by Brian Sedio, Juha-Pekka Salminen, Laura Prechi, Jack H. Dwenger, and Fiona T. Macneil
### Samples: *Orbicella faveolata* fragments from coral dissertation research in La Parguera, Puerto Rico

##### Freeze dry ground samples (8/11/21)
Samples were shipped from URI to UT Austin on dry ice and arrived frozen
1. Loosen caps of samples (may be necessary to thaw slightly)
2. Put in collector
3. Close and lock door, and close vent
4. Start it! (probably pre-set)
5. Will take roughly 24 hours to dry
6. Any samples that have not dried (likely due to a too tight cap), they can be dried again

##### Transfer samples (8/12/21)
1. Remove samples from freeze dryer
2. Use metal spatula to scrape inside of glass vial
3. "Marry" vial and labeled 2ml eppendorf tube (tube 1)
4. Turn upside down and tap dried sample into tube
5. Close tube!
6. Clean spatula between each sample using 90% ethanol solvent

##### Bash samples (8/12/21)
1. Put 2 steel beads in each sample tube
2. Load into tissue lyser
3. Bash for 1 min at ??????? Hz
4. Check to see if there is enough powder for later analysis
  - If necessary, bash again
5. Remove tube racks from lyser and tap down
6. Tap tubes to dump beads out
  - Beads can be cleaned and reused

##### Weigh out samples (8/13/21)
(Some samples were still wet on 8/12/21, process those)
1. Put labeled empty tube (tube 2) on scale
2. Tare
3. Use metal spatula to add sample to tube
4. Weigh
5. Add and remove sample as needed
6. Each sample should weigh 10 mg (+/- 0.5 mg)
7. Use kimwipe to clean spatula between samples
8. When finished, place leftover sample tubes in boxes and store in fridge

##### Suspend sample (8/13/21)
1. Pour solvent into tray
2. set autmatic pipet to 1.75 ml
3. Draw up solvent into pipet
4. Pump once to calibrate (it will stop flashing)
5. Fill each tube one by one
  - Do not let pipet tip touch anything!
  - Close current sample before opening next one to avoid cross-contamination
6. When finished, place samples in freezer

##### Shake samples (8/14/21)
1. Move samples from freezer to fridge
2. Morning: take out samples every hour and shake
3. Starting at lunch:
  - Put 24 samples in shaker
  - Shake for 30 min at 300 rpm
  - Switch samples
  - At every switch: shake all samples by hand
4. Place samples back in freezer

##### Supernatant extraction (8/15/21)
1. Remove samples from freezer
2. Shake samples by hand
3. Centrifuge at 12700 rpm for 10 min
4. Pipet out 1500 ul of supernatant into new labeled tubes (tube 3)
5. Save solid waste in cryobox in freezer
6. Place samples in tube 3 in freezer

##### Evaporate methanol (8/16/21)
1. Place tubes in SpeedVac
2. Open the lids!
3. SpeedVac for 1.5 hours
  - Check volume of tubes to determine if they are done
  - Don't let them dry out completely
4. Put small polyfill balls in the top of each tube
  - Do not let it touch the sample!
5. Put tubes in rack with caps "stuck down" (this will keep tubes from flying out during freeze drying)
6. Put racks in freeze drying
7. Turn on
8. It will probably only take a few hours to fully dry samples

##### Sample prep for MS (8/16/21)
1. Remove tubes from freeze-dryer, making sure they are fully dry
2. Remove polyfill balls and close
  - If necessary, you can freeze tubes at this stage to complete analysis later
3. Add 500 ul of solvent to each tube (be very careful about cross-contamination!)
4. Put samples in -20 freezer
5. (Some samples had to be re-freeze-dried overnight, and solvent was added the morning of 8/17/21)

##### Sample filtering (8/17/21 and 8/18/21)
1. Vortex samples if needed (only necessary if not homogenized, was not necessary for my samples)
2. Assemble syringe
3. Flush syringe with solvent at least 10 times
4. Draw up sample and discard empty tube (tube 3)
5. Remove needle
6. Clean fingers on kimwipe
7. Attach filter tip
8. Drip filter sample into new fully labeled tube (tube 4, final tube)
9. Discard filter tip
10. Replace needle and flush at least 3 times or until clear

##### Prep 96 well plates (8/17/21 and 8/18/21)
Samples should be in a 8x dilution
1. Add 10 ul water to each well
2. Add 2 ul of sample to each corresponding well
3. Make a data sheet recording the location of each sample
4. Ready to submit for MS!
