## Raker Lab RNA Extractions
### Performed by: Cassie Raker, Meagan Beaver, Khyara Perez, and Tommy Sah
#### Samples: *Orbicella faveolata* fragments from Dr. Raker's dissertation research in La Parguera, Puerto Rico (ciliate stress, growth, and acclimation)


## Protocol
Adapted from [Moynihan 2020](https://www.protocols.io/view/coral-tissue-and-skeleton-trizol-rna-extraction-bi9ikh4e.pdf),  [Medina et al. 2009](https://dmoserv3.whoi.edu/data_docs/SymBioSys/Coral-RNA-isolation-protocol-for-small-preps-in-1.5mL-or-2mL-tubes.pdf), and previous work by the Raker lab.

#### Materials needed
*All materials should be molecular grade: nuclease free, etc.*
* RNasZap (or similar)
* Homemade TRIzol reagent
* 100% Ethanol
* Nuclease free water
* 5M NaCl
* Soidum Citrate
* Glycogen (or GlycoBlue if available)
* Isopropanol
* Chloroform


#### Preparation
*In the hood!*
1. High salt buffer
    1. 5M NaCl: 2.4mL
    2. NaCitrate: 2.35g
    3. Nuclease-free water up to 10mL
2. Freshly prepare 75% ethanol
    1. 100% ethanol
    2. Nuclease-free water
    3. Place 75% ethanol in the 4C fridge until you need it

#### Protocol
*In the hood!*
1. Remove samples from freezer, let thaw on ice
2. Fill two 1.5mL nuclease-free tubes with RNase-free water (these are just for rinsing)
3. Place rinsed samples in empty bead bashing tubes
    * You can also create your own by adding glass beads to screw top tubes
4. Add 1mL of TRIzol
5. Place samples in Bead Blaster 24 (Carla's lab, 4th floor NLH)
    * Speed: max
    * Cycles: 5
    * Time: 1 min 30 sec
    * Interval: 5 sec
6. Run bead beater cycles
7. Centrifuge at 12000rcf for 10 minutes at 4C
8. Transfer 1mL of supernatant to new 1.5mL tube
9. Incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes
10. Add 200ul of chloroform
11. Shake sample vigorously by hand and vortex briefly
    * Sample should be milky
12. Centrifuge at 12000rcf for 15 minutes at 4C
13. Transfer upper clear aquaous phase to new 1.5mL tube
14. Add 250ul isopropanol
15. Add 250ul high salt buffer
16. Add 1ul glycogen (or GlycoBlue)
17. Vortext briefly
18. Incubate at room temperature for 10 minutes
19. Centrifuge at 12000rcf for 10 minutes at 4C
20. Remove and discard the supernatant
    * Do not disturb the pellet!
    * Pay attention to how your sample was positioned in the centrifuge: this will help you find the pellet
21. Add 1mL cold 75% ethanol to resuspend pellet
22. Vortex briefly
23. Centrifuge at 7500rcf for 5 minutes at 4C
24. Remove an discard supernatant
25. Add 1mL cold 75% ethanol to resuspend pellet
26. Vortex briefly
27. Centrifuge at 7500rcf for 5 minutes at 4C
28. Remove an discard supernatant
29. Leave lid open and air dry pellet for 5-10 minutes
30. Add 50uL nuclease free water
31. Pipette mix to resuspend pellet
32. Incubate at 55C for 10 minutes
33. Transfer to final lebeled tube
34. Aliquot 10uL for QC
35. Store RNA and aliquiot (if not using immediately) in -80 freezer in a clearly labeled box
